The Company believes that the political and economic risks related to the Company’s international operations are mitigated due to the geographic diversity in which the Company’s international operations are located.
As of March 31, 2018, the Company had no foreign currency forward exchange contracts outstanding.
The Company is exposed to market risk, including changes in interest rates.
The Company is subject to interest rate risk on its variable rate revolving credit facilities and term notes, which consisted of borrowings of $33.7 million at March 31, 2018.
A 100 basis point increase in the interest rate would have resulted in an increase in interest expense of approximately $.5 million for the three months ended March 31, 2018.
