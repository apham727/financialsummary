During the first nine months of 2019, the U.S. Dollar was stronger against most other currencies in which the Company pays its expenses.
In comparing average exchange rates during the first nine months of 2019 versus those during the same period of 2018, the Euro depreciated by 6%, the Pound depreciated by 6%, the Peso depreciated by 1% and the Renminbi depreciated by 5% against the U.S. Dollar.
Foreign exchange gains were also recognized in the first nine months of 2019 of $0.5 million on translation of local currency balance sheet accounts to the U.S. Dollar in consolidation, resulting from foreign currency fluctuations since December 31, 2018.
